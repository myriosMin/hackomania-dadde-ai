## Challenge Statement 

Community-Powered Emergency Funds with Instant Money Payouts

### Background

When natural disasters such as floods, typhoons, or earthquakes strike, financial aid is often slow, fragmented, or difficult to access. Yet communities already support one another. Neighbours, workplaces, diaspora groups, and schools often pool money informally to help members in need. Inspired by traditional ROSCAs (Rotating Savings and Credit Associations), this track explores how digital infrastructure and Open Payments can help communities:

• Collect small, recurring contributions in advance
• Define transparent fund rules collectively
• Automatically release financial support when disasters occur

Instead of relying on manual coordination or external aid, communities can prepare ahead of time and enable instant, programmable payouts during emergencies.

### The Challenge

Traditional emergency funds often fail because they are:
• Slow to distribute
• Dependent on manual approval
• Lacking clear payout rules
• Lack of transparency in how funds are allocated

As a result, people may wait too long for help or lose trust in the system.

There is an opportunity to design a system that:
• Enable small, flexible contributions (daily, weekly, or event-based)
• Automatically trigger payouts based on verified disaster events or location-based rules
• Apply clear and transparent distribution logic (e.g., equal split, severity-based, household size)
• Protect member data through secure, consent-based data handling
• Support community governance (e.g., voting on rules, thresholds, and allocation models)

### Challenge Statement

Community-Powered Emergency Funds with Instant Money Payouts

How might we design a programmable, community-driven emergency fund platform that leverages Open Payments to collect micro-contributions and automatically disburse financial aid based on verified disaster triggers and transparent payout rules?

Participants should design solutions that:
• Enable recurring micro-contributions using Open Payments
• Automatically trigger payouts based on trusted disaster signals (e.g., verified flood alerts, earthquake data, location-based triggers)
• Implement clear, programmable distribution logic (equal split, severity-based, household size, capped payouts, etc.)
• Protect member identity and financial data through secure, consent-based systems
• Support configurable governance mechanisms (e.g., voting on thresholds, allocation rules, trigger definitions)

### What Success Looks Like

A strong solution could demonstrate:

1. Instant & Automatic Payouts
• Money is sent automatically when disaster conditions are met
• Little to no manual coordination
• Clear and verifiable trigger rules

2. Clear & Transparent Tracking
• Real-time visibility into contributions and fund balances
• Clear tracking of who received payouts and under what rule
• Configurable dashboards (public or group-only)

3. Fair & Clear Rules
• Payout rules are defined upfront
• Community can adjust rules together
• System works whether the fund is small or large

4. Privacy & Respect
• Members can receive aid privately
• Personal hardship is not publicly exposed
• Secure handling of payments and identity

5. Built to Grow with the Community
• Adaptable to neighbourhoods, workplaces, diaspora groups, or schools
• Easy to join and contribute
• Low friction for both contributors and recipients
