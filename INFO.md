## Interledger Challenge: Community-Powered Emergency Funds with Instant Money Payouts

Community-Powered Emergency Funds with Instant Money Payouts

CHALLENGE STATEMENT

BACKGROUND

When natural disasters such as floods, typhoons, or earthquakes strike, financial aid is often slow, fragmented, or difficult to access.

Yet communities already support one another. Neighbours, workplaces, diaspora groups, and schools often pool money informally to help members in need.

Inspired by traditional ROSCAs (Rotating Savings and Credit Associations), this track explores how digital infrastructure and Open Payments can help communities:
- Collect small, recurring contributions in advance
- Define transparent fund rules collectively
- Automatically release financial support when disasters occur

Instead of relying on manual coordination or external aid, communities can prepare ahead of time and enable instant, programmable payouts during emergencies.

THE CHALLENGE

Traditional emergency funds often fail because they are:
- Slow to distribute
- Dependent on manual approval
- Lacking clear payout rules
- Lack of transparency in how funds are allocated

As a result, people may wait too long for help or lose trust in the system.

There is an opportunity to design a system that:
- Enable small, flexible contributions (daily, weekly, or event-based)
- Automatically trigger payouts based on verified disaster events or location-based rules
- Apply clear and transparent distribution logic (e.g. equal split, severity-based, household size)
- Protect member privacy through secure, consent-based data handling
- Support community governance (e.g. voting on rules, thresholds, and allocation models)



## Interledger Challenge: What Success Looks Like

Community-Powered Emergency Funds with Instant Money Payouts

CHALLENGE STATEMENT

WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

A strong solution could demonstrate:

1. Instant & Automatic Payouts
- Money is sent automatically when disaster conditions are met
- Little to no manual coordination
- Clear and verifiable trigger rules

2. Clear & Transparent Tracking
- Real-time visibility into contributions and fund balances
- Clear tracking of who received payouts and under what rule
- Configurable dashboards (public or group-only)

3. Fair & Clear Rules
- Payout rules are defined upfront
- Community can adjust rules together
- System works whether the fund is small or large

4. Privacy & Respect
- Members can receive aid privately
- Personal hardship is not publicly exposed
- Secure handling of payments and identity

5. Built to Grow with the Community
- Adaptable to neighbourhoods, workplaces, diaspora groups, or schools
- Easy to join and contribute
- Low friction for both contributors and recipients

## General Judging Criteria


JUDGING RUBRICS

Each challenge statement will have its own dedicated judging panel and pitching area. Teams will present directly to the judges assigned to their specific challenge. After all pitches are completed, each judging panel will select one top team from their challenge statement. This means you are competing within your problem track. Focus on solving your assigned challenge deeply and convincingly.

HackOMania is product focused. Judges are looking for real, working solutions that demonstrate impact. Strong storytelling helps, but the product must speak for itself.

Impact — 30%
Impact must be demonstrated through the product, not just claims. Judges will look for evidence that your solution can realistically improve lives or systems.
- Does the solution create meaningful positive impact?
- Who benefits and how real is that benefit?
- Is the AI for Good value clearly demonstrated and not just hypothetical?
- Is the problem relevant to society in Singapore?

Relevance — 15%
A well built solution that solves the wrong problem will not score well. Make sure your team understands the user, the context, and the urgency.
- Is the problem clearly defined and well understood?
- Does the built solution actually solve the stated problem?
- Is the solution appropriate for the intended users and context?
- Is this a problem worth solving now?

Solution Complexity — 20%
Simple and effective solutions can score highly. Unnecessary complexity will not. Use AI intentionally, not just because the theme says AI.
- Is the solution thoughtfully designed beyond a basic demo?
- Does it handle real world constraints, edge cases, or workflows?
- Is the architecture reasonable and well considered?
- Is AI used where it genuinely makes sense?

Product Execution — 35%
This carries the highest weight for a reason. HackOMania is about building. Judges want to see something that works, not just something that sounds impressive. Build real. Demo real. Let the product do the talking.
- Is there a working end to end product?
- Can judges see or interact with the solution live?
- Does the core functionality work reliably?
- Is this more than a presentation or mock up?
